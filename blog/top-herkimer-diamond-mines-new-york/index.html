Top Herkimer Diamond Mines to Visit in New York State

Herkimer Diamonds are a sparkling treasure found only in a few special places one of which is within the rocky layers of upstate New York. These dazzling quartz crystals, renowned for their natural double-terminated points and extraordinary clarity, have captivated rockhounds, geologists, and families alike especially because of their look-alike nature with diamonds.

The good news? You can dig for your own Herkimer Diamonds in several incredible mines across the beautiful state of New York!

In this article, we'll explore the top Herkimer Diamond mines to visit in New York, what makes them unique, the tools and tips you'll need, how you can plan your adventure to uncover these glimmering gems, and even look at other gems and crystals you can find in the Empire State. Whether you're a beginner, a seasoned rockhound, or a family looking for a fun day out, Herkimer mining is a must-try experience. Let's embark on this thrilling adventure!

What Are Herkimer Diamonds?

Herkimer Diamonds aren't actual diamonds—they're a unique variety of quartz crystal found primarily in Herkimer County, New York. What makes them special is their naturally formed points on both ends, called "double terminations." This rare feature makes Herkimer Diamonds look like they were hand-polished by nature!

These crystals formed millions of years ago in pockets of dolomite rock. Thanks to their stunning clarity and sparkle, they're treasured by rock collectors and jewelry makers around the world.

Here's what makes them so special:

  • Clarity: Their natural sparkle and transparency resemble true diamonds.
  • Formation: The precise geological process gives them their perfect shape.
  • Rarity: Their double-terminated feature is rare, making them highly prized by collectors.

Top Herkimer Diamond Mines in New York State

Let's explore the best places to mine for Herkimer Diamonds. Each location offers unique opportunities for adventure.

1. Herkimer Diamond Mines

Address: 4601 NY-28, Herkimer, NY 13350

This is the most popular destination for Herkimer Diamond mining, offering a wide range of activities for beginners and seasoned diggers alike.

What to Expect: A sprawling mining area with plenty of crystals to find.

Extras: Guided tours, a museum, and a gift shop filled with Herkimer Diamond jewelry.

Fees: Entry starts at $14 per person.

Pro Tip: Visit early in the day to beat the crowds.

2. Ace of Diamonds Mine & Campground

Address: 84 Herkimer St, Middleville, NY 13406

This mine is known for its friendly staff and a reputation for producing larger Herkimer Diamonds.

What to Expect: A hands-on mining experience with plenty of digging areas.

Extras: On-site camping, making it perfect for overnight trips.

Fees: Entry costs around $10-$15 per person.

Pro Tip: The best finds often require digging a little deeper, so bring sturdy tools!

3. Crystal Grove Diamond Mine & Campground

Address: 161 Co Rd 114, St. Johnsville, NY 13452

This mine offers a quieter, nature-focused experience.

What to Expect: Shaded digging areas, friendly staff, and a peaceful environment.

Extras: Well-maintained camping facilities for an immersive outdoor adventure.

Fees: Day passes start at $10.

Pro Tip: Look for smaller, loose rocks as they often contain hidden gems.

4. Treasure Mountain Diamond Mine

Address: Little Falls, NY

This lesser-known mine is a hidden gem for those looking to escape the crowds.

What to Expect: Smaller crowds and a chance to explore unique geological formations.

Extras: Guided tours available upon request.

Fees: Affordable entry fees around $10.

Pro Tip: Ask staff about the mine's history; it's fascinating!

5. Adirondack Mining Company

Address: Lake George, NY

This mine offers a blend of Herkimer Diamonds and other fascinating minerals.

What to Expect: Family-friendly activities and a variety of gem finds.

Extras: A gift shop with polished stones and jewelry.

Fees: Vary depending on activities chosen.

Pro Tip: Perfect for families with younger kids.

FAQs About Herkimer Diamond Mining

  1. Do I need a permit to mine for Herkimer Diamonds? No permit is needed at the commercial mines; entry fees cover access and digging rights.
  2. What should I bring for Herkimer Diamond mining? Bring a hammer, chisel, safety gear, gloves, and plenty of water. Some mines rent tools if you don't have your own.
  3. How do I clean Herkimer Diamonds after finding them? Use a toothbrush and soapy water to gently clean dirt and debris off the crystals.
  4. Are Herkimer Diamonds valuable? Yes! Their value depends on size, clarity, and overall quality, making them sought after for jewelry.
  5. Can I find other crystals while mining for Herkimer Diamonds? Yes, you might stumble upon calcite, dolomite, and other minerals.
