Top Alaska Rockhounding Dig Sites

Alaska offers incredible opportunities for rockhounds to explore the state's rich geological resources. If you're looking for a paid dig experience with amenities, Prospector John's and Crow Creek Gold Mine are two must-visit locations. Here's everything you need to know about these exciting destinations.

Prospector John's (Cooper Landing, AK)

Prospector John's is a renowned destination in Cooper Landing, Alaska, where visitors can immerse themselves in the tradition of gold panning. The camp offers all the tools you need to get started and expert guidance to ensure success. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced prospector, Prospector John's provides a hands-on, unforgettable adventure in the heart of Alaska's wilderness.

What You Can Find: Gold, garnets, fossils, and occasionally gemstones. Prospector John's is well-equipped for anyone looking to try their hand at panning and sluicing.

Beyond rockhounding, Prospector John's offers a range of other activities, including guided tours of Alaska's historical gold mining sites. This makes it a great destination for a well-rounded experience, perfect for families or groups seeking an adventurous day out.

Crow Creek Gold Mine (Girdwood, AK)

Crow Creek Gold Mine, located in the picturesque town of Girdwood, Alaska, is one of the most popular pay-to-dig sites in the state. Established in 1896, this historic site allows visitors to pan for gold in a scenic, rustic setting. The mine provides all necessary equipment, and their knowledgeable staff is on hand to assist, making it an ideal spot for families, beginners, and seasoned gold panners alike.

What You Can Find: Gold and gemstones. Crow Creek is known for the purity of its gold, making each pan you sift through a thrilling prospect.

In addition to gold panning, visitors can explore historical buildings, hike the trails surrounding the mine, and enjoy a picnic amidst the stunning Alaskan wilderness. It's the perfect combination of nature and adventure.

Best Time to Visit: Late spring through early fall is the best time to visit when the weather is mild and the streams are flowing at optimal levels for panning.
