Top Arizona Rockhounding Dig Sites

Arizona offers excellent rockhounding opportunities. One of the most popular pay-to-dig sites is Glitter Mountain, known for its beautiful selenite crystals. Learn more below!

Glitter Mountain (Littlefield, AZ)

Glitter Mountain, also known as Crystal Mountain or the Old Gypsum Mine, is a unique selenite mine located near the Arizona-Utah border. The mountain sparkles with gypsum crystals that make the area glimmer in the sunlight. It's a perfect spot for casual rockhounds and families looking to explore without venturing deep into a mine.

What You Can Find: Selenite (a transparent, crystal form of gypsum). Visitors can collect surface-level crystals around the pit.

Although Glitter Mountain has an active mining claim, visitors are welcome to collect loose selenite crystals around the site. Make sure to bring a small bucket, gloves, and hand tools like a chisel and hammer for light digging. It's important to respect the owner's request to avoid entering the pit itself.

Cost: There's no set fee, but the owners request payment for the selenite you take home. Payment can be made via Venmo or PayPal on-site.

Pro Tip: Bring water and wear sun protection, as there is no shade in the area. The site is accessible by dirt road, and a high-clearance vehicle is recommended.
