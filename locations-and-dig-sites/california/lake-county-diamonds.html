Rockhounding at Lake County

Welcome to Lake County, California, home to the remarkable Lake County Diamonds —quartz crystals that glitter like diamonds but are entirely unique in their formation. These stunning gems are found in the rolling hills surrounding Clear Lake, one of California’s largest natural lakes. These diamonds, known for their high clarity and occasional hints of color, are actually formed from volcanic glass created by the area's long-dormant volcanic activity millions of years ago. They may not be true diamonds, but their brilliance, double-terminated crystal structure, and relative rarity make them highly prized among rockhounds and collectors alike.

The history of Lake County Diamonds is as fascinating as the crystals themselves. The volcanic activity that formed these gems is tied to the ancient eruption of Mt. Konocti, a massive volcanic mountain that towers over the southern shore of Clear Lake. The heat and pressure from the volcanic eruption caused silica to crystallize into these gem-like formations, creating the beautiful quartz crystals that rockhounds seek today.

Lake County offers an incredible opportunity for both beginner and seasoned rockhounds to explore, discover, and collect these dazzling gems. Whether you’re walking along the shores of Clear Lake or hiking up the volcanic hills of Mt. Konocti, there’s always a chance you’ll stumble upon one of these natural treasures.

What Can Be Found at Lake County in California

Lake County is rich in unique geological finds, with Lake County Diamonds being the main attraction. However, that’s not all you might uncover in this picturesque area:

  • Lake County Diamonds: These clear quartz crystals resemble diamonds in their brilliance. They are often double-terminated and can range from small pebbles to larger, more significant specimens. Occasionally, you might find some with a hint of color, like pale yellow, pink, or brown.
  • Obsidian: Black volcanic glass, often found in regions surrounding volcanic activity, can also be found in Lake County. Obsidian is sharp and glossy, and while not as rare as the diamonds, it adds another exciting element to your rockhounding adventure.
  • Agate: Although less common, agates of various colors and patterns can sometimes be found around Clear Lake, adding another beautiful collectible to your haul.
  • Fossils and Petrified Wood: Lake County has areas where ancient plant fossils and petrified wood can be discovered, especially near Borax Lake. These finds provide an incredible glimpse into California’s prehistoric past.

Location of Lake County Diamonds, California

Lake County Diamonds can be found scattered across the landscape around Clear Lake and Mt. Konocti. These diamonds are primarily located in volcanic basalt rocks and deposits that were left behind by ancient eruptions. Here are some prime locations to explore:

    Clear Lake Shorelines: One of the easiest spots for beginners is right along the shores of Clear Lake, particularly after a rainstorm when the soil has been loosened, making it easier for the diamonds to surface. Try sifting through the sandy patches along the water's edge.

    Mt. Konocti Hiking Trails: The mountain’s slopes are littered with opportunities to discover Lake County Diamonds. The higher you go, the more challenging the terrain, but also the greater your chance of uncovering larger, more pristine crystals. The hiking trails around Mt. Konocti provide breathtaking views and excellent rockhounding opportunities.

    Borax Lake: This small, shallow lake is a treasure trove of geological history. It’s known for its deposits of volcanic minerals, including Lake County Diamonds. Fossils and petrified wood have also been found here, making it a perfect destination for those seeking more than just diamonds.

How to Get There

Lake County is located approximately two hours north of San Francisco, making it a convenient day trip for many rockhounds. The area around Clear Lake is accessible by car, with parking available near popular hiking spots like Mt. Konocti County Park and public access points along Clear Lake’s shore. A detailed map can be obtained from local visitor centers, which provide precise directions to the best collecting areas.

Where to Collect

Once you’ve arrived, the hunt begins! Popular collecting areas include the trails around Mt. Konocti and the High Valley Ridge, both of which are easily accessible. Always make sure to bring plenty of water, a hat for sun protection, and your rockhounding gear.

Lake County is a public area, so no reservations are required. However, be mindful of private property boundaries and always check local regulations before setting out.

Recommended Gear and Tips for Lake County Diamonds

Rockhounding in Lake County requires preparation and the right tools. Here’s a list of recommended gear to ensure a successful and enjoyable adventure:

  • Sturdy Footwear: Whether hiking the hills or walking along the shore, good shoes are essential. The terrain can be rocky and uneven, especially near Mt. Konocti.
  • Rock Hammer and Chisel: Bring these tools to help break apart volcanic rock to uncover Lake County Diamonds. Some of the best finds are nestled inside the hard basalt.
  • Sifting Screen: A screen or sifter is an invaluable tool for separating the diamonds from dirt and debris. This is especially useful along the lake’s shorelines.
  • Buckets: Always bring a few buckets to carry your treasures back home. Five-gallon buckets work best.
  • Protective Gloves: The volcanic rocks can be sharp, so wear gloves to protect your hands while digging and handling rocks.
  • Sun Protection: The area can get quite hot during the summer, so bring a hat, sunscreen, and plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Backpack: A sturdy backpack to carry your gear, food, and water is essential, especially if you plan to hike into the hills.
  • More importantly, be patient and have fun collecting!

Rockhounding Tips For Lake County Diamonds

The best time to search is immediately after a rainfall. The rain loosens the soil and washes away dirt, revealing the hidden diamonds on the surface.

While some diamonds are embedded in volcanic rocks, many can be found on the surface. Walk slowly and keep an eye out for glints of light on the ground. Collecting Lake County Diamonds can take time, but the effort is worth it when you find those sparkling gems.

Methods for opening gems include the following:

  • Breaking and smashing: You can place a gem in a cloth sack or similar and smash it on a hard surface. This may break the gem into many small pieces.
  • Hammer and chisel or center punch: This will open your gem with a rough or uneven edge.
  • Sawing: You can cut a gem with a rock or similar saw. Most tools with a carbide-tipped blade will cut a gem. This works well if you also want to polish it.
  • Pipe cutter: You can use a tool called a soil pipe cutter which uses a chain with blades to cut the gem as if it were a pipe. Tools like this can sometimes be rented from home improvement stores.

Please be careful while digging, the soft material you are digging in can collapse at any time. Tunneling is unsafe and not permitted.

Other Dig Sites in California

California is a fantastic destination for rockhounding enthusiasts. With its diverse geological features and rich deposits of various minerals and gemstones, the state offers numerous dig sites that cater to both beginners and experienced rockhounds. From stunning benitoite to vibrant tourmaline, California's dig sites provide an exciting and rewarding experience for all.

Benitoite Mining Company

The Benitoite Mining Company is a renowned destination for rockhounding enthusiasts, known for its stunning benitoite crystals and beautiful scenery.

Pala Chief Mine

The Pala Chief Mine offers a unique opportunity to find vibrant tourmaline crystals and other beautiful gems in the historic Pala mining district.

Oceanview Mine

The Oceanview Mine is known for its rich deposits of tourmaline, kunzite, morganite, and other beautiful gems.
