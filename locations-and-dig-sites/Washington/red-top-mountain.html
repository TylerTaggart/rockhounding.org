About Red Top Mountain

Red Top Mountain, situated in the Wenatchee National Forest in Washington State, is a beloved destination for rockhounding enthusiasts. This area is celebrated for its diverse mineral deposits, making it a popular destination for both novice and seasoned collectors. The geological history of Red Top Mountain provides a fascinating context for the array of minerals and gemstones that can be unearthed here.

Visitors to Red Top Mountain can expect to discover a wide range of agates and jaspers, with stunning colors and patterns, as well as beautiful quartz crystals. The primary attraction at Red Top Mountain is the abundant agates found in various colors and patterns. These agates can be found as nodules scattered across the surface or within the rock formations. Accessing the agate beds usually requires a high-clearance vehicle due to the rough and rocky terrain. It's also important to be prepared for a hike, as the collecting areas may be some distance from where you park.

What Can Be Found At Red Top Mountain

At Red Top Mountain, rockhounds can find a variety of gemstones and minerals, including:

  • Agate: Known for its colorful and banded patterns.
  • Jasper: Found in various hues, adding vibrant colors to any collection.
  • Quartz Crystals: Including clear, smoky, and occasionally amethyst.
  • Chalcedony: Often found in nodules or seams within the host rock.
  • Other Minerals: Rarer finds may include amethyst and even petrified wood.

Location of Red Top Mountain

Red Top Mountain is located in the Wenatchee National Forest in Washington. The exact coordinates are 47.2670° N, 120.7572° W. The area is accessible by car, with several marked trails leading to prime rockhounding spots.

The mountain offers breathtaking views and scenic trails, providing a serene environment for rockhounding. Several public lands are available for exploration, and visitors are encouraged to respect the natural surroundings while searching for agates, jaspers, and other minerals.

Recommended Gear and Tips

When visiting Red Top Mountain, it's important to come prepared. Here are some recommendations for your trip:

  • Full-Day Exploration: Plan to stay for at least a full day to fully explore the area and increase your chances of finding valuable gems.
  • Essentials: Bring plenty of water, snacks, and sun protection, as the area can get quite hot, and there are limited facilities nearby.
  • Safety Gear: Wear sturdy shoes and protective gloves to prevent injuries from sharp rocks and tools.
  • Tools: Carry tools such as rock hammers, chisels, and safety glasses to safely extract and handle the minerals you find.

Rockhounding Tips for Red Top Mountain

  • Trail Exploration: Many minerals can be found along the established trails, so be sure to explore them thoroughly.
  • Hillside Slopes: Concentrate your search on hillside slopes and areas where erosion is evident, as agates and jaspers tend to accumulate in these locations.
  • Look for Patterns: Agates and jaspers often have distinctive colors and patterns, so keep an eye out for these characteristics.
  • Creek Beds: Focus on the creek beds where water flow exposes minerals embedded in the gravel and sand.
  • Gentle Extraction: Use a rock hammer to carefully chip away at larger rocks to uncover minerals hidden within. Always wear safety glasses to protect your eyes from flying debris.

Before you head out, research the area thoroughly. Look for information online, in rockhounding guides which you will see at the bottom of this page, and from other rockhounds who have visited Red Top Mountain.

GPS coordinates and information from other rockhounds can help locate the different areas where most gems are located. Even if you don't locate the specific agate beds, exploring the area around Red Top Mountain can still yield rewarding finds. Keep an eye out for exposed rock faces, loose rocks, and areas where others have been digging.

Other Dig Sites in Washington

Washington is a fantastic destination for rockhounding enthusiasts. With its diverse geological features and rich deposits of various minerals and gemstones, the state offers numerous dig sites that cater to both beginners and experienced rockhounds. Here are some other recommended sites:

Hansen Creek

Hansen Creek, located in the Cascade Mountains, is a prime destination for quartz crystal enthusiasts, offering a variety of quartz types, including clear, smoky, and amethyst.

Walker Valley

Near Mount Vernon, Walker Valley is renowned for its rich deposits of geodes, containing minerals such as quartz, agate, and calcite.

Stonewall Creek

Stonewall Creek, in the Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest, is a well-known location for finding petrified wood and agate.
