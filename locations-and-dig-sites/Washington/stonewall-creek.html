About Stonewall Creek

Stonewall Creek, nestled in the Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest in Washington State, is a renowned destination for rockhounding enthusiasts. The Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest is a vast area known for its geological diversity. This area is celebrated for its rich deposits of petrified wood and agate, making it a popular spot for both amateur and experienced collectors. The geological history of Stonewall Creek provides a unique context for the array of minerals and gemstones that can be unearthed here.

While specific information about Stonewall Creek is limited, the surrounding region is known for various minerals and rocks. Visitors to Stonewall Creek can expect to discover not only petrified wood but also a variety of agates and other minerals. The natural beauty of the region and the excitement of finding your own unique specimens make Stonewall Creek a cherished spot for rockhounding adventures.

Rockhounding at Stonewall Creek offers a rewarding experience for those interested in geology and the natural world. What Can Be Found At Stonewall Creek

At Stonewall Creek, rockhounds can find a variety of minerals and gemstones, including:

  • Petrified Wood: The primary attraction, with beautifully preserved specimens.
  • Agate: Known for its colorful and banded patterns.
  • Jasper: Occasionally found, adding vibrant colors to any collection.
  • Chalcedony: Often found in nodules or seams within the host rock.
  • Quartz Crystals: Including clear and smoky varieties.
  • Other Minerals: Rarer finds may include common opal and quartz crystals.

Location of Stonewall Creek

Stonewall Creek is located in the Okanogan-Wenatchee National Forest in Washington. The exact coordinates are 48.0195° N, 120.7300° W. The area is accessible by car, with several marked trails leading to prime rockhounding spots.

The creek flows through a scenic forested area, offering a serene and picturesque environment for rockhounding. Several public lands are available for exploration, and visitors are encouraged to respect the natural surroundings while searching for petrified wood and other minerals.

Recommended Gear and Tips

When visiting Stonewall Creek, it's important to come prepared. Here are some recommendations for your trip:

  • Full Day Exploration: Plan to stay for at least a full day to fully explore the area and increase your chances of finding valuable specimens.
  • Essentials: Bring plenty of water, snacks, and sun protection, as the area can get quite hot, and there are limited facilities nearby.
  • Safety Gear: Wear sturdy shoes and protective gloves to prevent injuries from sharp rocks and tools.
  • Tools: Carry tools such as rock hammers, chisels, and safety glasses to safely extract and handle the minerals you find.

Rockhounding Tips for Stonewall Creek

  • Surface Searching: Many petrified wood pieces and agates can be found on the surface, especially after rain, which washes away loose soil and reveals hidden gems.
  • Creek Beds: Focus on the creek beds where water flow exposes minerals embedded in the gravel and sand.
  • Look for Patterns: Petrified wood and agates often have distinctive colors and patterns, so keep an eye out for these characteristics.
  • Gentle Extraction: Use a rock hammer to carefully chip away at larger rocks to uncover minerals hidden within. Always wear safety glasses to protect your eyes from flying debris.
  • Erosion Areas: Concentrate your search on areas where erosion is evident, as minerals tend to accumulate in these locations.
  • Check Gravel Bars: Explore gravel bars and areas where the creek has deposited sediment, as these are prime locations for finding treasures.

The creek bed itself is a prime spot to search for agates and other minerals. Look for exposed gravel bars, areas where water has eroded the banks, and around larger rocks. Explore the hills and slopes around the creek. Agates and other minerals can often be found weathering out of the soil or exposed in rocky outcrops.

The best time to visit is during the drier months (summer and fall) when the water levels are lower, and more of the creek bed is exposed. Before you head out, research the area and learn about the geology and specific types of minerals found in the Stonewall Creek area. This will help you identify potential finds.

Other Dig Sites in Washington

Washington is a fantastic destination for rockhounding enthusiasts. With its diverse geological features and rich deposits of various minerals and gemstones, the state offers numerous dig sites that cater to both beginners and experienced rockhounds. Here are some other recommended sites:

Hansen Creek

Hansen Creek, located in the Cascade Mountains, is a prime destination for quartz crystal enthusiasts, offering a variety of quartz types, including clear, smoky, and amethyst.

Walker Valley

Near Mount Vernon, Walker Valley is renowned for its rich deposits of geodes, containing minerals such as quartz, agate, and calcite.

Liberty Gold Mine

The Liberty Gold Mine, near Cle Elum, offers opportunities for gold panning and finding quartz crystals with gold inclusions.
