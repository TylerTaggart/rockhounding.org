Rockhounding At The Dinosaur Ridge

Dinosaur Ridge, located near Morrison, Colorado, is one of the most famous dinosaur fossil localities in the world. Known for its well-preserved dinosaur tracks and bones, the ridge attracts visitors, paleontologists, and fossil enthusiasts from around the globe.

The site is part of the Morrison Fossil Area National Natural Landmark and offers a glimpse into the Late Jurassic period. Dinosaur Ridge features exposed layers of rock where visitors can see fossilized footprints and bones of dinosaurs such as Apatosaurus, Stegosaurus, and Allosaurus.

The main attractions include the Dinosaur Bone Quarry, where dinosaur bones can be seen embedded in the rock, and the Dinosaur Tracksite, which showcases numerous tracks made by dinosaurs walking through the area millions of years ago.

The tracksite is famous for its extensive dinosaur tracks, which include footprints from both herbivorous and carnivorous dinosaurs. Visitors can see these tracks up close, offering a unique opportunity to step back in time and walk where the dinosaurs once roamed.

Dinosaur Ridge has a rich history of fossil discovery, dating back to the late 19th century when the first dinosaur bones were found in the area. Since then, it has become a significant site for paleontological research and public education.

In addition to the tracks and bones, the site offers educational programs, guided tours, and a visitor center with exhibits on the area's geology and paleontology. The Friends of Dinosaur Ridge, a nonprofit organization, manages the site and provides resources for visitors to learn about the history and significance of the fossils found here.

Visitors can explore the ridge through self-guided tours or join guided hikes to learn more about the different fossil sites. The visitor center provides maps, guides, and educational materials to enhance the experience.

The area is also known for its stunning views of the surrounding landscape, making it a great destination for both fossil enthusiasts and nature lovers.

What Can Be Found At Dinosaur Ridge

At Dinosaur Ridge, visitors can find a variety of dinosaur fossils and tracks, including:

  • Dinosaur Tracks: The primary attraction, with numerous well-preserved tracks from different dinosaur species. These tracks provide valuable insights into the behavior and movement of these ancient creatures.
  • Dinosaur Bones: Fossilized bones of dinosaurs such as Apatosaurus, Stegosaurus, and Allosaurus can be seen embedded in the rock. These bones offer a direct connection to the dinosaurs that once roamed the area.
  • Plant Fossils: In addition to dinosaur remains, plant fossils from the Jurassic period can also be found, providing a glimpse into the ancient ecosystems that existed alongside the dinosaurs.

Location of Dinosaur Ridge

Dinosaur Ridge is located near Morrison, Colorado, and is easily accessible from the Denver metropolitan area. The coordinates for the main visitor center are:

Latitude: 39.6769° N

Longitude: -105.1978° W

Visitors can use these coordinates to navigate to the site and explore the rich fossil heritage of Dinosaur Ridge.

Recommended Gear and Tips

When visiting Dinosaur Ridge, it is important to be prepared with the right gear to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience. Recommended gear includes:

  • Sturdy Hiking Boots: The terrain at Dinosaur Ridge can be rocky and uneven, so proper footwear is essential.
  • Sunscreen and Hat: The site is exposed to the sun, and it is important to protect yourself from sunburn and heat.
  • Plenty of Water: Staying hydrated is crucial, especially during hot weather.
  • Backpack: To carry your essentials, including maps, guides, and any souvenirs or samples you might collect.
  • Binoculars: For viewing distant features and wildlife in the area.

Visitor Center: Start your visit at the Dinosaur Ridge Visitor Center to learn about the site's history and the fossils found here.

Guided Tours: Consider taking a guided tour for a more in-depth understanding of the geology and paleontology of Dinosaur Ridge.

Trails: Explore the various trails, such as the Dinosaur Ridge Trail and the Triceratops Trail, to see the dinosaur tracks and other geological features.

Exhibits: Visit the exhibits to see displays of dinosaur bones and learn about the different types of dinosaurs that once lived in the area.

Gift Shop: Stop by the gift shop to find souvenirs and educational materials related to dinosaurs and paleontology.

Other Dig Sites in Washington

While collecting fossils at Dinosaur Ridge is not permitted, Colorado offers other opportunities for rockhounding and fossil hunting:

Mount Antero

Mount Antero is renowned for its aquamarine crystals, making it a top destination for gem hunters. The area also offers smoky quartz and topaz.

Pikes Peak

Pikes Peak offers diverse geological formations and a variety of minerals, including amazonite, quartz, and topaz, making it a favorite among rockhounds.

Crystal Peak

Located near Florissant, Crystal Peak is famous for its amazonite and smoky quartz combinations, providing a picturesque backdrop for rockhounding.
