Top Colorado Rockhounding Dig Sites

Colorado's diverse geological landscape offers an abundance of rockhounding opportunities. From quartz crystals to rare minerals, these dig sites are perfect for rockhounds of all levels.

Crystal Peak

Located near Florissant, Crystal Peak is famous for its abundance of smoky quartz and amazonite. The area's rich deposits make it a top destination for rockhounds looking to add beautiful specimens to their collection.

Be sure to bring appropriate tools like shovels and rock hammers, and be prepared for a challenging but rewarding dig. The best time to visit is in the late summer when the weather is favorable, and the trails are easily accessible.

Devils Head

Devils Head, located in the Pike National Forest, is renowned for its pegmatites, which contain quartz, feldspar, and beryl. This site is perfect for rockhounds seeking unique and large specimens.

The rugged terrain offers a challenging adventure, so bring sturdy footwear and plenty of water. The trails are well-marked, making it accessible to both beginners and experienced collectors.

Dinosaur Ridge

Dinosaur Ridge, near Morrison, Colorado, is a must-visit for fossil enthusiasts. The site offers an array of dinosaur tracks and fossils, making it a popular destination for both amateur and seasoned paleontologists.

To fully enjoy the experience, consider joining a guided tour that provides insights into the area's rich prehistoric history. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture the stunning geological formations and fossil finds.

Lake George

Lake George is well-known for its spectacular amazonite and smoky quartz crystals. The area's rich history of mining offers an exciting opportunity to find these prized minerals.

When planning your visit, be sure to bring the necessary tools and equipment, as the area can be challenging to navigate. The rewards, however, are well worth the effort.

Learn More

Mount Antero

Mount Antero, located in the Sawatch Range, is famous for its aquamarine deposits. This high-altitude site offers a challenging yet rewarding experience for rockhounds.

Given the elevation, it's important to be well-prepared with the right gear and to acclimate properly. The stunning views and the possibility of finding aquamarine make it a top destination.

Learn More

Pikes Peak

Pikes Peak is known for its rich deposits of amazonite and smoky quartz. The area has been a popular rockhounding destination for years, offering stunning specimens for those willing to dig.

Access to Pikes Peak can be challenging due to its altitude and terrain, so ensure you are well-equipped with the necessary tools and supplies. The beautiful landscape and potential finds make the effort worthwhile.
